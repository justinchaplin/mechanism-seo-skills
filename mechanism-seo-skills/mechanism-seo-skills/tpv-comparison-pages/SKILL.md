# TPV Comparison & Competitor Pages

This skill builds comparison and roundup content for TPV properties and PortCo content strategies. These pages serve dual purposes: they rank for category keywords, and they drive CTR2 — clicks from a TPV page to a PortCo landing page. Both goals shape how these pages are designed.

The CTR2 filter is the most important principle: **the correct question is "is this reader persuadable toward the PortCo?" not "does the PortCo make this exact product?"** A reader comparing laundry pods is still in the detergent consideration set and can be converted to a laundry sheets recommendation. A reader comparing enterprise CRM platforms cannot be converted to a $30 consumer product.

---

## Page Types

### 1. "Best [Category]" Roundup (most common for TPV)
The anchor page for a category. Ranks multiple products/brands, with the PortCo featured prominently as the editorial recommendation.

- **Target keyword**: `best [category]`, `best [category] [year]`
- **Structure**: Intro → Top Pick callout → Ranked list with mini-reviews → Comparison table → Buying guide → FAQ
- **CTR2 placement**: PortCo as #1 or Best Overall pick, with a clear CTA and affiliate/direct link

### 2. "X vs Y" Comparison Page
Head-to-head between two specific products. High purchase intent, lower volume than roundups. Good for capturing mid-funnel buyers.

- **Target keyword**: `[Product A] vs [Product B]`, `[Product A] vs [Product B] [year]`
- **Structure**: Quick verdict → Side-by-side table → Deep dive per product → Who should buy which → Final recommendation
- **CTR2 placement**: Verdict section and final recommendation drive toward PortCo

### 3. "Alternatives to X" Page
Readers already know a competitor brand and are shopping around. Very high purchase intent.

- **Target keyword**: `[Competitor] alternatives`, `best alternatives to [Competitor]`
- **Structure**: Why look for alternatives → Ranked list of alternatives (PortCo first or second) → Quick comparison table → Verdict
- **CTR2 placement**: PortCo as top recommendation with clear differentiation from the named competitor

### 4. Category Format Coverage (e.g., detergent formats)
Extends TPV authority across product sub-categories. Captures readers at different points in the category.

- **Target keywords**: `best [format] detergent`, `best laundry pods`, `best liquid detergent`
- **Structure**: Format-specific intro → Top picks → Comparison table → Format-specific buying guide
- **CTR2 placement**: Depends on PortCo overlap — apply the persuadability filter

## CTR2 Persuadability Filter

Before including a product category on a TPV page, apply this test:

**Question**: "Is a reader of this page in a consideration mindset where the PortCo's product could be a legitimate alternative?"

| Example | Pass/Fail | Reasoning |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Pods page → recommend laundry sheets | ✅ Pass | Reader is mid-consideration for laundry care; sheets are a genuine format alternative |
| Liquid detergent page → recommend laundry sheets | ✅ Pass | Same category, same problem, sheets solve it differently |
| "Best industrial laundry detergent" → sheets | ❌ Fail | Commercial/industrial buyer has completely different requirements |
| "Best Tide Pods flavor" → anything else | ❌ Fail | Reader is already committed to Tide Pods specifically |
| "Best putting mats" → PrimePutt mat | ✅ Pass | Direct category alignment |
| "Best full golf simulators" → putting mat | ❌ Fail | Completely different product category and price point |

If the category fails the persuadability filter, don't build the page as a CTR2 vehicle — it's only worth building if it has standalone SEO value for the TPV as a category authority site.

## Keyword Research Process

1. **Identify the category** and the PortCo's position within it
2. **Map the SERP landscape** for the primary "best [category]" keyword — what page types are ranking? (roundups, brand sites, retailer pages)
3. **Identify sub-category and comparison variants**:
   - Format variants: `best [format] [category]`
   - Comparison pairs: `[PortCo brand] vs [Competitor]`
   - Alternatives: `[Top competitor] alternatives`
   - Use case: `best [category] for [use case]`
4. **Prioritize by**: Search volume × CTR2 persuadability × SERP winnability

**Title Tag Formulas:**
- Roundup: `[N] Best [Category] in [Year] — Tested & Ranked`
- Vs: `[A] vs [B]: Which Is Better? ([Year])`
- Alternatives: `[N] Best [A] Alternatives in [Year] (Tested)`

## Comparison Table Design

Feature matrices are the highest-engagement section of comparison pages. Build them with genuine differentiation — not just checkmarks.

**For ecommerce/CPG (laundry, household goods):**
| | [PortCo] | [Competitor A] | [Competitor B] |
|--|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Format | Sheets | Pods | Liquid |
| Scents available | 4 | 12 | 8 |
| Loads per pack | 40 | 35 | ~45 |
| Price per load | $0.28 | $0.35 | $0.22 |
| Plastic-free | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| HE compatible | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Rating | ⭐ 4.7/5 | ⭐ 4.5/5 | ⭐ 4.2/5 |

**For performance/equipment (golf, fitness):**
| | [PortCo] | [Competitor A] | [Competitor B] |
|--|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Size | 9" × 16" | 12" × 18" | 6" × 12" |
| Surface texture | Tour-grade felt | Astroturf | Foam |
| Slope adjustment | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Price | $89 | $149 | $39 |
| Warranty | 2 years | 1 year | 6 months |

**Rules:**
- All pricing must have an "as of [month year]" note
- Flag which product you tested vs. which you researched from public sources
- Never invent feature data — verify from manufacturer pages or direct testing

## Schema Markup for Comparison Pages

### ItemList (for roundup pages)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "ItemList",
  "name": "Best [Category] [Year]",
  "description": "[Brief description of the list]",
  "itemListOrder": "https://schema.org/ItemListOrderDescending",
  "numberOfItems": [N],
  "itemListElement": [
    {
      "@type": "ListItem",
      "position": 1,
      "name": "[Product Name]",
      "url": "[Product URL or review section anchor]"
    },
    {
      "@type": "ListItem",
      "position": 2,
      "name": "[Product Name]",
      "url": "[URL]"
    }
  ]
}
```

### Product + AggregateRating (for individual product sections)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Product",
  "name": "[Product Name]",
  "description": "[Product description]",
  "brand": { "@type": "Brand", "name": "[Brand]" },
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "[e.g. 4.7]",
    "reviewCount": "[Number of reviews]",
    "bestRating": "5",
    "worstRating": "1"
  }
}
```

**Do NOT use FAQ schema.** FAQ schema is restricted to government and healthcare sites as of August 2023. Standard Q&A formatting is fine — just no JSON-LD FAQ markup.

## CTR2 Conversion Optimization

These pages serve two masters: Google (rankings) and the PortCo (revenue). Design for both.

### CTA Placement
- **Above the fold**: PortCo "top pick" callout with link before the ranked list
- **Within the PortCo review section**: Prominent "Buy Now" or "Check Price" CTA
- **After the comparison table**: "Our recommendation: [PortCo]" with link
- **Final verdict section**: Closing recommendation with clear PortCo CTA
- Avoid aggressive CTAs in competitor sections — it breaks trust and reads as paid placement

### Trust Signals (essential for CTR2 conversion)
- "Last updated [month year]" timestamp — comparison pages go stale fast
- Editorial methodology disclosure — how were products selected and tested?
- Disclose which product is the PortCo — transparency builds trust, and readers often know anyway
- Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly — if you only say good things about the PortCo, readers discount the whole page

### Internal Linking from TPV to PortCo
- Link to the PortCo's direct product or category page (not just the homepage)
- Use descriptive anchor text: "Hey Sunday laundry sheets" not "click here"
- Where appropriate, link to a PortCo landing page with UTM tracking for CTR2 attribution

## Fairness Guidelines

These pages work because readers trust them. Don't abuse that trust.

- **Verify all competitor claims** from public sources (manufacturer pages, retailer listings, review sites)
- **Never make false claims** about competitors — not "Competitor X has bad quality" without evidence
- **Acknowledge competitor strengths** — if Competitor A is genuinely better at something, say so
- **Disclose PortCo relationship** — readers should know which product is "ours"
- **Keep pricing current** — stale pricing destroys credibility; update at least quarterly
- **No defamation** — opinion is fine, false statements of fact are not

## Output

Save the report as a Word document (.docx) using python-docx.

**Filename:** `[portco]-[skillname]-[M.DD.YY].docx`
**Example:** `liveitup-aeogeoaudit-3.13.26.docx`
**Save location:** `~/Documents/mechanism-seo/outputs/`

Structure the document in this exact order:

---

**[Skill Name] — [Brand] — [Date]**

**TL;DR**
2–3 sentences. The headline finding and the single most important action.

---

**Summary**
Narrative overview written for a CEO. What was audited, what was found, overall health or status. No bullet points, no data tables. Plain language. 3–5 paragraphs.

---

**Findings**
Full analysis — all data, tables, competitive breakdowns, page-level detail, and supporting evidence. Nothing stripped out. This is the working reference for whoever is executing the work.

---

**Action Items**
Numbered list. Each item states the action and why it matters or what impact it drives.

1. [Action] — [why it matters / expected impact]
2. [Action] — [why it matters / expected impact]
3. [Action] — [why it matters / expected impact]
